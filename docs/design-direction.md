# Wakefield Design Direction

Wakefield should feel like a quiet digital western: a local signal station for an always-on Codex assistant.

## Visual Idea

- Digital desert landscape, not generic AI glass.
- Blocky or voxel geometry, used sparingly and with large readable shapes.
- Sand, mesas, horizon light, and small signal particles as the recurring visual language.
- Friendly mystery over mascot energy.

## Palette

- Keep the app UI on native macOS system colors by default.
- Let the app icon carry the digital western palette: ink sky, mesa clay, cactus shadows, warm sand, and small neon signal particles.
- Use custom brand colors only for future illustration or marketing surfaces, not routine controls.

## UI Rules

- Keep the macOS app mostly native and calm.
- Prefer system accent and system status colors for controls, selection, and state.
- Avoid purple-blue AI gradients, glossy chatbot clichés, and busy decorative backgrounds.
- Prefer large simple icon shapes over miniature scenes when an asset must read small.
